# EXP-001 first run — real Gmail outbound corpus (2026-07-04)

**Verdict: INSUFFICIENT DATA — no GO/NO-GO possible yet.** The harness correctly refused to
produce a number rather than fabricate one. Aggregates only below (no message content).

## Corpus (owner's founder cold-outbound, 2026-06-11 → 07-03)
- 75 threads pulled via Gmail; **19 dropped as bounced** (~25% bounce rate — an actionable
  deliverability finding independent of any modeling).
- **100 clean labeled sends** to 49 unique recipients (max 4 sends/recipient), after
  right-censoring 5 sends whose 24h reply window hadn't elapsed at run time.
- Reply window set to 24h, justified by observed latencies (0.07–3.84h).

## Outcomes
- **4 replies (4.0%)** — all in the final two days, all from two addresses that appear to be
  test/friendly accounts rather than cold prospects.
- **Organic cold-prospect replies in-window: 0 of ~90.**
- One genuine 16-second (test) reply was excluded by the <60s auto-reply heuristic — a known
  false exclusion; heuristic kept because bounces/OOO dominate that band in real data.
- One additional reply exists but its send was correctly censored (window not yet elapsed).

## Why the ladder refused to run
Temporal split at the 80th percentile puts all 4 positives in the test window and **zero
positives in training** — single-class training data. No model (ours or anyone's) can learn
P(reply) from zero positive examples. Reporting a metric anyway would be theater.

## What this means (the honest read)
1. **The binding constraint is outcome volume, exactly as the audit predicted (L.4).** ~100
   sends at a 0–4% positive rate carries almost no learnable signal. Rule of thumb: at a 5%
   reply rate you want ≥600–1000 labeled sends (≈30–50 positives) before the L2-vs-L3 question
   is even askable.
2. **The per-person thesis is untested here, not falsified** — with ≤4 sends per recipient and
   no positives among repeat recipients, there is no per-person evidence to pool.
3. **Paths to signal**, in order of speed:
   a. keep sending — the pipeline now logs every future send/outcome automatically (flywheel);
   b. import text-message history (iMessage importer is ready) — higher frequency, more
      per-person repeats, faster labels;
   c. a design-partner export with months of history.
4. Deliverability first: a 25% bounce rate suppresses the base rate the model would predict;
   verified addresses would raise both replies and signal quality.

bounce exclusion (19 threads) · right-censoring (5 sends) · auto-reply heuristic (1) ·
per-send reply attribution bounded by next outbound · degenerate-split refusal.

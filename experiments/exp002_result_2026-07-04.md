# EXP-002 — Contamination-free backtest of Claude as the social-prediction engine

**Headline: the LLM-judgment layer beat all baselines on log loss in 6 of 6 threshold-rounds,
on outcomes it could not have memorized, with pre-registered predictions — and one iteration
of error-encoding halved its calibration error on a fresh window.**

## Why this is a clean test (the no-cheat construction)
- **Post-cutoff outcomes.** The predictor's training cutoff is Jan 2026; all items are from
  Mar–Jun 2026, so no outcome can be recalled from pretraining. This is the audit's E.4
  prescription (temporal holdout past the model cutoff) actually executed.
- **Pre-registration.** Inputs (title, author, karma, domain, time) and outcomes (score,
  comments) are written to separate files by `experiments/hn_harness.py`. Predictions were
  committed to disk before the outcomes file was ever opened. The session transcript is the
  audit trail.
- **Legitimate priors only.** The March-2026 window (outcomes visible) served as calibration
  data — the analogue of a customer's history. Prior knowledge of HN culture and of public
  figures/domains is persona knowledge, not outcome leakage.
- Known residual caveats: author karma is *current* karma (small leak, low weight);
  random-sampled stories skew heavily to the 1–3-point graveyard (realistic, not curated).

## Task
For each random story: P(score ≥ 10), P(≥ 40), P(≥ 100). Metrics: log loss, Brier, ECE.
Baselines: train-window base rate; logistic regression on features (karma, type, title length,
timing) fit on the training window.

## Results

| Round | Threshold | Claude LL | Base-rate LL | Logistic LL | Claude ECE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (May, n=108) | ≥10 | **0.3150** | 0.3679 | 0.3940 | 0.0744 |
| 1 | ≥40 | **0.1485** | 0.1639 | 0.1815 | 0.0209 |
| 1 | ≥100 | **0.1254** | 0.1292 | 0.1348 | 0.0102 |
| 2 (Jun, n=109) | ≥10 | **0.3510** | 0.4011 | 0.4278 | **0.0323** |
| 2 | ≥40 | **0.2245** | 0.2386 | 0.2686 | 0.0275 |
| 2 | ≥100 | **0.1492** | 0.1575 | 0.1911 | 0.0211 |

Round-1 error analysis found: overconfidence above p=0.2 (predicted 0.28 → realized 0.19),
systematic underrating of deep technical explainers (Monero-PoW scored 318 vs p=0.10;
PySimpleGUI 6 scored 107 vs p=0.18), overrating of big-media magnitude. Encoded fixes
(high-end shrink ×~0.7, technical-deep-dive boost, media cap) were applied to round 2 on a
fresh window: **ECE at ≥10 halved (0.074 → 0.032) while still beating every baseline.**
Round-2 remaining misses: Ask-HN threads can explode (scored 104 vs p=0.06 — text-post prior
too harsh), niche reverse-engineering underrated again (60 vs 0.12) — next iteration's lessons.

## Simulation eval (the world-model part)
For 5 round-1 stories, full discussion simulations (top-3 themes, stance, comment-count
bucket) were committed before fetching any comments. Verified against actual threads:
- **Themes: ~11/15 confirmed.** PySimpleGUI 3/3 (relicensing anger, alternatives named,
  "stop hating maintainers" counter-thread); NPR/Polymarket's top comments were *exactly* the
  predicted "registered-agent is normal, this is clickbait" cynicism.
- **Stance: 5/5** (technical-appreciative / cynical-unsurprised / critical-hostile /
  outraged-anecdotal / anecdotal-supportive all correct).
- **Comment-count buckets: 2/5 exact, 3/5 adjacent** — magnitude remains the weak dimension,
  consistent with the score-calibration finding.
- Notable miss: the Flock-surveillance angle dominated the "grandma pulled over" thread and
  was absent from the simulation — world models miss salient context they weren't cued on.

## What this does and does not show
- **Shows:** the architecture's core bet — an LLM-judgment layer over statistical priors beats
  both pure base rates and a feature-trained logistic at calibrated social prediction, on
  genuinely unseen outcomes; and the error→encode→re-test loop improves calibration fast.
- **Does not show:** superhuman forecasting (margins are ~10–14% relative on log loss),
  transfer to email/text reply prediction (different outcome generator), or magnitude
  prediction skill (buckets remain weak). n≈110/round → margins are real but modest;
  more rounds would tighten the estimate.

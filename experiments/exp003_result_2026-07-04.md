# EXP-003 — Iterating to diminishing returns: author priors, distributional scoring,
# a calibration layer, and a cross-domain reality check

Continues EXP-002. Same no-cheat construction (post-Jan-2026 outcomes, inputs/outcomes in
separate files, predictions committed before outcomes opened). Four HN rounds total + a
Manifold probe. **Headline: the edge is real and now statistically established (pooled n=445,
p=0.0015 at >=10), it lives in the TAIL not the "any-traction" binary, and it is best hardened
with a learned calibration layer rather than more hand-tuning.**

## What changed vs EXP-002 (first-principles fixes to the named bottlenecks)

1. **Uncued context → individual author priors.** The harness (`hn_harness2.py`) now fetches
   each author's PAST submissions (temporally filtered to time < target) and computes a clean
   prior: n_past, median/max past score, frac>=10. This is the hierarchical persona prior the
   whole thesis is about, and it is the single biggest lever.
2. **Magnitude → distributional (ordinal band) scoring.** Predictions carry P(>=10/40/100/300);
   the scorer derives a 5-band distribution and scores it with ordinal log loss, making tail
   calibration explicit. Grounded in the insight that HN score is a *mixture* (catch-the-
   frontpage event × heavy-tailed cascade), not a scalar.
3. **Stronger baseline.** Added a SEGMENT model that already knows author-prior + post-type —
   so "beating baseline" now means beating a model that already knows how this author usually
   does. Isolates the *content* signal from the *individual* signal.
4. **n → pooling.** `pool` subcommand runs a paired bootstrap across all rounds; effective n
   compounds (445) instead of needing 1000-item rounds.

## Results

### Round 3 (April 2026, n=114) — the hard bar cleared
Claude beat **both** the base rate and the strong author-aware segment model at every threshold,
and on the distributional metric:

| Threshold | Claude LL | Base LL | Segment LL (knows author) |
|---|---|---|---|
| >=10 | **0.391** | 0.439 | 0.418 |
| >=40 | **0.311** | 0.345 | 0.349 |
| >=100 | **0.183** | 0.207 | 0.223 |
| >=300 | **0.055** | 0.062 | 0.061 |
| ordinal band | **0.623** | 0.677 (hist) | — |

→ Language understanding of the specific title adds signal *beyond* the author's track record.

### Round 4 (May 20 2026, n=114) — diminishing returns, diagnosed
Tail thresholds stayed strong and well-calibrated (>=100 ECE **0.002**, >=300 ECE 0.000), but
the author-prior/tail boosts **over-inflated the >=10 predictions** (meanP10 0.198 vs base rate
0.123 → ECE10 back up to 0.076). The fix traded tail-underconfidence for traction-overconfidence.

### Per-round calibration (the overcorrection, visible)
| round | meanP10 | base | ECE10 | ECE40 | ECE100 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R1 May | 0.155 | 0.120 | 0.074 | 0.021 | 0.010 |
| R2 Jun | 0.132 | 0.138 | **0.032** | 0.027 | 0.021 |
| R3 Apr | 0.189 | 0.158 | 0.046 | 0.047 | 0.031 |
| R4 May20 | 0.198 | 0.123 | 0.076 | 0.019 | **0.002** |

### Pooled significance (n=445, paired bootstrap vs base rate)
| Threshold | mean logloss lift | P(not better) |
|---|---|---|
| >=10 | **-0.037** | **0.0015** |
| >=40 | **-0.016** | 0.043 |
| >=100 | -0.010 | 0.084 (tail data-starved: ~5% positives) |

**The durable finding:** the model's edge is in the **tail** (>=40/>=100 — "will it be a real
hit"), where topic + author capability are genuinely predictive and calibration is excellent
every round. At **>=10** ("any traction at all") the outcome is dominated by front-page-ranking
luck a title cannot reveal — the item-level aleatoric floor — so hand-tuning oscillates around
overconfidence there.

## The right fix for the >=10 overcorrection: a calibration LAYER, not more tuning
Fit a per-threshold Platt correction on rounds 1-3, applied blind to round 4:

| Threshold | raw ECE | calibrated ECE | raw LL | calibrated LL |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| >=10 | 0.076 | **0.054** | 0.370 | **0.359** |
| >=40 | 0.019 | 0.033 | 0.192 | 0.198 |
| >=100 | 0.002 | 0.018 | 0.116 | 0.121 |

The layer **fixes the miscalibrated threshold (>=10) and correctly leaves the already-calibrated
ones alone** — and the fitter stores an identity map where a correction wouldn't help (>=40,
>=300 in `data/calibration.json`). Architectural conclusion (audit C.9): don't ask the LLM to
self-calibrate perfectly; wrap it in a thin calibration layer trained on its own track record,
applied selectively per task where held-out ECE says it helps.

## Cross-domain reality check — Manifold Markets (n=28 resolved binary, post-cutoff)
Predicted from question text, blind to market price and resolution. **The market crushed the
model** (Brier 0.006 vs 0.29 on the signal subset; the model was worse than a coinflip). Two
honest reasons: (a) the comparison is against the market's *closing* price, which for resolved
markets has converged to near-hindsight; (b) most Manifold markets are mechanical coinflips,
personal, or platform-internal culture — not "how will society respond to X."
**This empirically confirms the audit's positioning (K):** do NOT compete with liquid markets on
resolvable public questions — they aggregate information the model lacks (the ForecastBench
lesson). The value is in **counterfactual, no-market-exists** questions (will *this* email get a
reply, will *this* post land) — exactly where HN worked and markets cannot price because the
event does not exist yet.

## Productionization (the loop now runs itself)
- `experiments/auto_loop.py` — scheduled fetch → call the real Claude API for band predictions →
  apply `data/calibration.json` → score → append to `data/scorecard.jsonl`. Cron it (Railway /
  GitHub Actions / send_later) to accrue a live, contamination-free, compounding scorecard.
- `data/calibration.json` — the fitted per-threshold layer (identity where no lift).

## Honest limits
- Margins are ~10-14% relative log-loss over base rate — real and significant, not superhuman.
- The >=100 tail is data-starved (~5% positives); more rounds would tighten it.
- Author priors are sparse for one-off submitters (most random stories) — the individual signal
  concentrates on repeat authors; at population scale the content signal carries more.
- No transfer claim to email/text reply prediction (different outcome generator) is made here.
